  • Global warming

    Global warming is a gradual increase in the earth’s temperature generally due to the greenhouse effect caused by increased levels of carbon dioxide, CFCs, and other pollutants.

    The is a phenomenon of a gradual increase in the temperature near the earth’s surface. This phenomenon has been observed over the past one or two centuries.

    However, the concept of global warming is quite controversial but the scientists have provided relevant data in support of the fact that the temperature of the earth is rising constantly

Causes of Global Warming

Man-made Causes of Global Warming:

  • Deforestation

    the action of clearing a wide area of trees.

    The release of large amounts of stored carbon dioxide into the atmosphere when trees are cut down

  • Use of Vehicles

    Cars release greenhouse gases (GHGs) like carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide into the atmosphere

  • Industrial Development

    Changing from a farming based economy to one that uses machines and factories.

    Also releases large amounts of greenhouse gases, primarily carbon dioxide, into the atmosphere through the burning of fossil fuels used to power factories and machinery, thus trapping heat and raising the planet's temperature

  • Agriculture

    Cows and other ruminant animals produce large quantities of methane, a powerful greenhouse gas, through their digestive processes.

    Applying nitrogen-based fertilizers can lead to the release of nitrous oxide, another potent greenhouse gas.

    Clearing forests to create farmland releases large amounts of stored carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.

  • Overpopulation

    Drives up the demand for resources like energy and food, leading to increased greenhouse gas emissions from activities like deforestation, industrial production, and transportation

  • Chlorofluorocarbon (CFC)

    Nontoxic, nonflammable chemicals containing atoms of carbon, chlorine, and fluorine (Old refrigerants)

    • Natural Causes of Global Warming:

  • Volcanoes

    by releasing carbon dioxide (CO2), a greenhouse gas, into the atmosphere

  • Water Vapor

    Water vapor traps heat in the atmosphere, which warms the planet and contributes to global warming

  • Melting Permafrost

    releasing large amounts of stored carbon dioxide and methane, potent greenhouse gases, into the atmosphere

  • Forest Blazes

    An uncontrolled fire that spreads rapidly through a forested area, fueled by dry vegetation and often intensified by wind conditions

    Fires emit CO2 and other greenhouse gasses

    • Effect of global warming

  • Rise in Temperature

    As greenhouse gas emissions blanket the Earth, they trap the sun's heat

  • Threats to the Ecosystem

    causing species loss, changing weather patterns, and damaging habitats.

  • Spread of Diseases

    mosquitoes will roam beyond their current habitats, shifting the burden of diseases like malaria, dengue fever, chikungunya and West Nile virus.

  • High Mortality Rates

    Due to more frequent and severe heat waves, storms, and floods

  • Loss of Natural Habitat

    changing weather patterns and rising sea levels.

    • Ozone Layer Depletion

    Ozone layer depletion is the gradual thinning of the earth’s ozone layer in the upper atmosphere caused due to the release of chemical compounds containing gaseous bromine or chlorine from industries or other human activities.

      The ozone layer is found in the lower portion of the earth’s atmosphere.

      It has the potential to absorb around 97-99% of the harmful ultraviolet radiations coming from the sun that can damage life on earth.

      If the ozone layer was absent, millions of people would develop skin diseases and may have weakened immune systems.

      However, scientists have discovered a hole in the ozone layer over the Antarctica.

      This has focussed their concern on various environmental issues and steps to control them.

      The main reasons for the ozone hole are chlorofluorocarbons, carbon tetrachloride, methyl bromide and hydrochlorofluorocarbons

      Ozone layer depletion is the thinning of the ozone layer present in the upper atmosphere.

      This happens when the chlorine and bromine atoms in the atmosphere come in contact with ozone and destroy the ozone molecules.

      This happens when the chlorine and bromine atoms in the atmosphere come in contact with ozone and destroy the ozone molecules.

      One chlorine can destroy 100,000 molecules of ozone. It is destroyed more quickly than it is created.

      Some compounds release chlorine and bromine on exposure to high ultraviolet light, which then contributes to the ozone layer depletion.

      Such compounds are known as ozone depleting substances (ODS).

      The ozone-depleting substances that contain chlorine include chlorofluorocarbon, carbon tetrachloride, hydrochlorofluorocarbons, and methyl chloroform.

      Whereas, the ozone-depleting substances that contain bromine are halons, methyl bromide, and hydro bromofluorocarbons.

      Chlorofluorocarbons(CFC's) are the most abundant ozone-depleting substance.

      It is only when the chlorine atom reacts with some other molecule, it does not react with ozone.

      Montreal protocol was proposed in 1987 to stop the use, production and import of ozone-depleting substances and minimise their concentration in the atmosphere to protect the ozone layer of the earth.

    Man-made sources of the Ozone layer depletion

  • Chlorofluorocarbons

    when they slowly rise into the stratosphere, are broken down by strong ultraviolet radiation, release chlorine atoms, and then react with ozone molecules

  • Unregulated Rocket Launches

    emits greenhouse gasses into the stratosphere, where the ozone layer resides, which can then react with and destroy ozone molecules

    • Natural sources of the Ozone layer depletion

  • Sun-spots and stratospheric winds

    influencing the distribution and reactivity of ozone-depleting substances (ODS) like chlorine atoms in the stratosphere.

  • volcanic eruptions
  • Nitrogenous Compounds
